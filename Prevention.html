Prevention is always the best way. Maintaining digital hygiene can help keep you safe and have a meaningful & positive internet & social media presence.


For Parents...

While nothing can guarantee that our children will not be cyber-bullied, there are many things parents can do to lessen the likelihood that it will happen and to minimize its effects if it does occur.

1. Listening

Listening is probably the most important tool we can use when helping our children to deal with bullying – or most anything else, for that matter. By listening, rather than leading the conversation, we give our child the time, attention and security necessary to get to the bottom of the real issues.

2. Discussing

We need to keep the conversation going with our children regarding the dangers – and fun – of the internet.

3. Teaching Internet Safety

-Talk about the 4P’s – privacy, predators, pornography, pop-ups.
-Find out about their online friends – who are they? Do they treat your child with respect?
-Tell them not to give out any personal details or post information that they would not want EVERYONE to know.
-Be up front – let them know that you will investigate files, buddy lists, online activities, review postings.


For children...


Here are simple things your child can do to stay safe online and avoid cyberbullying:

1. Accept only people he knows as online friends

If child adds someone he doesn’t really know as a ‘buddy’, ‘friend’ or ‘follower’, it gives that person access to information about child that could be used for bullying.

2. Don’t give out passwords.

Some teenagers give their passwords to friends as a sign of trust, but a password gives other people the power to pose as your child online.

3. Tell you, a teacher or another trusted adult if he’s worried

about anything that’s happening online, including if he sees someone else being cyberbullied.


For Teens...


Victims of cyberbullying can experience symptoms of depression including sadness, loneliness, insecurity, poor self-esteem, academic decline, feelings of not belonging, and suicidal thoughts and behavior.

1. Educate

Educate yourself on cyberbullying and figure out the best way to address it– this will help you to be prepared if it ever occurs.

2. Don’t respond or retaliate

Ignore messages from people you don’t know. The internet can be a great place to make new friends but it is still super important to be extra cautious due to fake accounts and trolls

3. Save the evidence

The only good news about bullying online or on phones is that it can usually be captured, saved, and shown to someone who can help. You can save that evidence in case things escalate
